Debut-Present

On October 4th 2021 Star news reported that Yuehua Entertainment would soon be debuting a new boy group. The name of the group would be "Tempest" and the former duo HyeongseopXEuiwoong would both be part of the line up. The groups original debut date was February 21st 2022, but after all members tested positive for covid 19 the debut was post poned till March 2nd. On March 2nd Tempest debuted with the mini album "It's ME, It's WE" with the title track "Bad news" On August 11 2022 Tempest unveiled a promotional schedule for their second mini album, "Shining Up", which would release on August 29. Later on November 7, they released a teaser for their third mini album, "On and On", scheduled to drop on November 22.In 2023 on March 30, Tempest released a teaser for their upcoming comeback, scheduled for April 17. The next day, it was confirmed that Tempest would be releasing their fourth mini album, "The Calm Before The Storm". On June 20th their first solo concert was announced to be held in Japan. On August 31, Tempest released a teaser for their upcoming comeback which is set for release on September 20. The title of their first single album, "Into The TEMPEST", was revealed the next day. On October 19th Yuehua announced that Hwarang would go on hiatus after under going surger on his injured shoulder, and had to recover. On January 3, Tempest released a mysterious 'coming soon' teaser. A few days later, they announced that their pre-debut Japanese digital single, 'Baddest Behavior,' would be released on January 31. On February 8, Tempest announced their first fan concert, scheduled for April 13 and 14 at Yonsei University Auditorium. Shortly after, on February 16, they released a teaser poster for their fifth mini album, 'TEMPEST Voyage,' set to be released on March 11. Nippon Columbia later confirmed TEMPEST's Japanese debut with the mini album 'Bang!' on April 10. However, on March 6, Yue Hua Entertainment announced that Hwarang would halt activities with the group due to trust issues between both parties. On July 10th it was announced that Tempest would compete on the survival show Road to Kingdom:Ace of Ace. On August 11th Yuehua announced that after a mutual agreement, Hwarang would be leaving the group and his contract would be terminated.The group would then continue activities with the remaining six members. On October 8, Tempest announced the release of their second Japanese mini album, initially set for December 6 and later revealed to be titled 'Bubble Gum.' However, the release date was subsequently moved to December 11.
